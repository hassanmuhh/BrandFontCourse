    Meet your instructor

    Folmer Rieniee Fayler enjoys experimenting with typography, developing typefaces, images, and initials that push the boundaries of type technology. His typefaces create stories of their own and are more than just a collection of characters. Each keystroke becomes a separate narrative because the stories are stored in font files that can be loaded on any computer and utilised by anyone. He does customised work through his type foundry, Typearture, for companies like Adobe. His retail typefaces have received honours from the Type Directors Club and Communication Arts. Don't forget to look at his Instagram. "The designer of variable colour fonts who is absolutely out of control!" — Ulrich Rausch

    - Which font drawing tool do I need?

    +

    - A vector drawing program like Illustrator, Figma or Affinity Design.
    - A pre-installed version or free trial of Glyphs 3. Note: Glyphs is only available for Mac. Note: Glyphs Mini does not support variable or color fonts.

    - What software skills do I need?

    +

    You know how to draw in a vector based program like Illustrator, Figma or Affinity Design. In the course we use Illustrator. Nice to have but not necessary: basic knowledge of HTML, CSS and Javascript.
